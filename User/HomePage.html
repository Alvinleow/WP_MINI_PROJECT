Climate change is a global crisis that demands immediate action. Greenhouse gas emissions have risen by over 50 percent since 1990, leading to unprecedented global warming and devastating consequences. Extreme weather events, economic losses, and human suffering are escalating.

Sustainable Development Goal 13 (SDG 13) - Climate Action - aims to address this urgency. It seeks to mobilize $100 billion annually to support developing countries in adapting to climate change and transitioning to low-carbon development. By integrating disaster risk reduction, sustainable resource management, and human security, we can advance not only SDG 13 but also other Sustainable Development Goals.

It is crucial to limit global temperature rise to 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, with a target of 1.5 degrees Celsius. This requires collective action, political will, increased investment, and technology utilization. Together, we can mitigate climate change, protect vulnerable communities, and create a sustainable future.
